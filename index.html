Careers and opportunities

Are you looking for ways to use your unique skills and education to help make government produce better results that benefit Canadians? Canada’s Impact and Innovation Unit (IIU) has three (3) different ways you can work with our team and partners across government to start making a difference.

1. Fellowship

Recruitment of subject matter experts to join government to lead on priority projects. Fellows work jointly with the IIU and departments/agencies who are seeking advice and expertise in the areas of:

  • Behavioural insights
  • Impact investing (Innovative finance)
  • Impact measurement
  • Data science

As a fellow, you bring your expertise and leadership skills to the table and help grow a culture of innovation within government that ultimately leads to better results for Canadians.

The Impact Measurement stream will open in April 2018. The Data Science stream will open in Spring 2018. Notices will be posted on this website, as well as on Twitter and LinkedIn. Follow us @impact_innovEN to keep abreast of application deadlines!

Get in touch with us

2. Affiliates

As part of our project work, the IIU team frequently seeks out the guidance and expertise of academics and subject matter experts in behavioural insights, design, impact investing (innovative finance), impact measurement and data science.

As an Affiliate, your work will be used to improve government policies and programs, while giving you the opportunity to publish results.

If you are interested in becoming an Affiliate, contact us and tell us about your area of expertise.

Get in touch with us

3. Policy and program entrepreneurs

The Policy and Program Entrepreneurship supports the Deputy Minister Task Force on Public Sector Innovation (TF-PSI). This cross-departmental committee is dedicated to accelerating transformative approaches into how programs are delivered to the public, and draws on the imagination and dedication of public servants who want to see policy done differently.

Public servants seeking to grow as leaders and expand their experience have the unique opportunity to collaborate with their deputy minister on priority projects that fall under the TF-PSI mandate.
